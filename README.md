# 🛒 Walmart Customer Purchase Behavior Analysis

An end-to-end statistical analysis project using Confidence Intervals, Bootstrapping, the Central Limit Theorem (CLT) and Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to evaluate customer spending behavior and generate data-driven marketing insights.

---

## Project Overview

This project analyzes Walmart customer purchase transactions to understand spending patterns across demographic segments. Using statistical inference techniques including Confidence Intervals, Bootstrapping and the Central Limit Theorem (CLT), the analysis estimates population-level purchasing behavior and supports evidence-based business decisions.

---

## Business Problem

Walmart wants to determine whether customer demographics such as gender, age and marital status significantly influence purchase behavior. The goal is to identify meaningful customer segments for targeted marketing and personalized promotional strategies.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze customer purchase behavior across demographic groups.
- Apply Bootstrapping to estimate population spending distributions.
- Construct 95% Confidence Intervals for customer segments.
- Validate population inference using the Central Limit Theorem.
- Compare spending behavior across gender, age and marital status.
- Generate statistically supported business recommendations.

---

## Dataset

- Domain: Retail
- Records: 550,068 customer transactions
- Features: Customer demographics, product information and purchase amount.

---

## Data Preparation

- Verified data completeness with no missing values.
- Performed outlier analysis while retaining genuine high-value purchases.
- Conducted univariate and bivariate exploratory data analysis.
- Prepared demographic segments for statistical inference.

## Key Business Insights

- Male customers demonstrate higher average purchase values, with confidence intervals consistently above female spending levels.
- Customers aged 26–35 represent the largest purchasing segment, while older age groups exhibit higher average spending.
- Confidence intervals for married and unmarried customers overlap significantly, indicating marital status has minimal impact on spending.
- Purchase distribution is positively skewed, with a small group of high-value customers contributing disproportionately to revenue.
- Bootstrapping and CLT provide reliable population-level estimates, supporting statistically confident business decisions.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Prioritize marketing campaigns for customers aged 26–35 to maximize revenue potential.
- Develop gender-specific promotions based on statistically significant spending differences.
- Reward high-value customers through loyalty programs and personalized offers.
- Focus customer segmentation on age and gender rather than marital status.
- Apply statistical insights to improve customer targeting and promotional planning.
